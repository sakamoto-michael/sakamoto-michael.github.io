About

Developers' Foundation

Outside of classroom projects, my first developer experience was with the Developers' Foundation (at Queen's University).

Mentored by the team at the Western University branch, I gained experience in planning, developing, and deploying polished webpages (for non-profit organizations). I worked together with clients to develop reasonable requirements for each project. I learned not only about fundamental web development skills, but also about design principles, professional client interaction, and team-oriented thinking to empower other organizations.

Celestica Inc.

Entering the industry, I became an intern of the IT Analytics team at Celestica, a multinational business-to-business corporation.

To say that this internship experience was valuable would be an understatement. There were endless learning opportunities. I was able to collaborate with both local and international teams from various departments on impactful projects. I pushed for various responsibilities, including coding for the company's analytics platform, project managing, training users, as well as being an intermediary between the analytics team and business/supply chain teams. I also partook in Celestica's mentorship program, where I learned about project and time management skills, as well as programming with modern technologies to solve diverse company problems.
